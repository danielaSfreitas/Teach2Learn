Intermediate

At this level, you are no longer just a student; you are becoming a mentor. You will learn to describe past events and connect ideas to give clear explanations to others.

1. Essential Grammar: Past Simple (Regular Verbs)

To tell a story or give a lesson about the past, you need to change the verbs. For regular verbs, we usually add -ED:

Verb (Present) Past Simple Meaning (Significado)
To Work Worked Trabalhou / Trabalhei
To Study Studied Estudou / Estudei
To Play Played Jogou / Joguei / Brincou
To Help Helped Ajudou / Ajudei

Example: "Yesterday, I studied English for two hours."

2. TALKING ABOUT EXPERIENCES

How to describe your weekend

In the intermediate level, you need to connect your past actions. Use these expressions to teach someone about your day:

  • Last weekend... - (No último final de semana...)
    Ex: "Last weekend, I visited my family."
  • I stayed at home - (Eu fiquei em casa)
    Ex: "I was tired, so I stayed at home."
  • It was amazing! - (Foi incrível!)
    Ex: "I watched a movie and it was amazing."
  • I learned something new - (Eu aprendi algo novo)
    Ex: "I studied hard and I learned something new."

Tip: Try to repeat these sentences thinking about your last vacation.

Exercises

1- Change the verb to the Past Simple:

I (work) ________ very hard yesterday.

2- Answer in English: What did you do last weekend?

3- Vocabulary Check: What is the past of "Help"?

Practice: Try to say out loud: "I helped my friend with the homework."

3. Creative Challenge: The Mentor Lesson

Imagine you are teaching a friend about your past experiences. Write a short lesson (3-5 lines) explaining what you did yesterday or last weekend. Try to use at least two verbs in the past!
